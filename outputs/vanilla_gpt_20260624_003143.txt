=== LLM OUTPUT ===

The Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) is a formula used to determine the optimal order quantity that minimizes the total inventory costs, which include ordering costs and holding costs. The EOQ formula is given by:

\[
EOQ = \sqrt{\frac{2DS}{H}}
\]

Where:
- \( D \) is the annual demand (10,000 units in this case),
- \( S \) is the ordering cost per order ($50 in this case),
- \( H \) is the holding cost per unit per year ($2 in this case).

Plugging in the values:

\[
EOQ = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 10000 \times 50}{2}}
\]

\[
EOQ = \sqrt{\frac{1000000}{2}}
\]

\[
EOQ = \sqrt{500000}
\]

\[
EOQ \approx 707.11
\]

Therefore, the Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) is approximately 707 units.
